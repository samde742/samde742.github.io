Example: Computers

Summary

  • The enery waste from computers contrubutes to global warming
  • A computer in sleep mode still uses energy
  • A computer turned off but plugged in draws small amounts of energy from your outlet
  • They create a strain on the electrical grid if many of them are being powered at once
  • The average PC uses 746 kilowatts per year (246kw than a fridge)

Statistics

  • 1.3 billion people(including businesses) own and operate computers(2019) --> 1.3b times 746kw
  • A desktop computer will use 200w of power per hour
  • A computer left on for 8 hours a day for a year would amount to 175 kg of CO2 emissions
  • In stand-by(sleep) mode your computer will produce approximately a third of this

In General

Summary

  • Electronics can contribute substantial amounts of CO2 emissions per year
  • An electronic classifies as something that requires power to run
  • An electronic left in sleep mode will always continue to use power
  • Different types of energy being used to power computers can produce more or less emissions
  • E-waste also plays a big part in electronic pollution and emmissions
    • E-waste are categorized as unwanted electronics --> in turn get thrown away
    • The electronics contain materials which when burnt accumulate in the air and release CO2(Copper and Iron for example)

Statistics

  • Electronics make up 3.7% of carbon emissions(approx 14 oz per year) --> this is expected to skyrocket to 14% by 2040
  • Roughly 50 devices and appliances are always drawing power(Even when shutdown or in sleep mode)
  • 12.5% of e-waste is actually recycled
  • 20-50 million metric tons of ewaste are produced per year
  • 530LBS of fossil fuel, 48LBS of chemicals and 1.5 tons of water are needed to manufacture a computer monitor
